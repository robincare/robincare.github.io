"Check Your Mate"

Cancer prevention and education from the Prevent Cancer Foundation

Prevent Cancer Foundation is a site dedicated to stopping cancer before it starts through prevention and education. The site is fun, engaging, and inclusive of the LGBTQ+ community. One section, called Check Your Mate reaches out to millennials, people born between 1982 and 2004, about cancer prevention. When you’re young, it’s easy to think that cancer won’t affect you. The Prevent Cancer Foundation is raising awareness through playful photo cards with instructions on how to check your “mate” for the three most common cancers: skin, testicular, and breast.

The pictures draw them in and the catch phrases keep them engaged and more likely to share the resource with their friends and romantic partners. Here are some of the quippy (yet incredibly useful) reminders that the best way to show you love someone is by helping check for cancer.

“Baby please, let me give them a squeeze.”

According to the Prevent Cancer Foundation, more than 252 thousand women and 2,400 men are diagnosed with breast cancer each year. Here’s what they recommend you look for when checking yourself (or your partner):

  • A lump, hard knot or thickening in the breast
  • A lump under your arm
  • A change in the size or shape of a breast
  • Nipple pain, tenderness or discharge, including bleeding
  • Itchiness, scales, soreness or rash on nipple
  • A nipple turning inward or inverted
  • A change in skin color and texture (dimpling, puckering or redness)
  • A breast that feels warm or swollen

You can also check out Robin Care’s guide to identifying the warning signs of breast cancer.

“Be my bestie, check my testes.”

Though testicular cancer is usually curable with early detection, approximately 410 men will die from the disease this year alone. The Prevent Cancer Foundation lays out these guidelines on what men and their partners should check for:

  • A painless lump, enlargement or swelling in either testicle
  • A change in how the testicle feels
  • Dull aching in the lower abdomen, back or groin
  • Pain or discomfort in a testicle or in the scrotum
  • Sudden collection of fluid in the scrotum
  • Feeling of heaviness in the scrotum

“Make them grin, check their skin.”

No one wants skin cancer but many millennials aren’t as proactive as they need to be about protecting their skin. The Prevent Cancer Foundation wants men, women, and their partners to be on the lookout for:

  • A sore that doesn’t heal
  • A mole or other growth you haven’t noticed before
  • Change in the border of a spot, spread of color, redness or swelling around the area
  • A small, smooth, shiny, pale or waxy lump that may bleed
  • Large areas with oozing or crust
  • A flat red spot or a lump that is scaly or crusty
  • Itchiness, tenderness or pain from a mole or elsewhere on your skin
  • A brown or black colored spot with uneven edges

These are all signs of skin cancer that should be checked out by a doctor.

No matter which generation you belong to, you can probably relate to feeling invincible. But no one is immune to cancer and it’s important that we all work to raise awareness about prevention. Early detection is the best way to protect yourself (and the ones you love) from cancer.
